## What the Images Show

### The Subject

Both the reference and reproduced trajectories render a vivid Bauhaus-inspired image: primary-colored geometric shapes (circles, triangles, rectangles, irregular polygons) arranged in a dense, overlapping composition on a white ground. Blues, yellows, reds, greens, and pinks dominate. The composition is busy and flat, consistent with the Bauhaus aesthetic of the prompt.

### The Diffusion Process Across Steps

**step_00** (the initial pure noise latent): Both ref and repro decode to identical images — visually indistinguishable television static. The comparison stats confirm this: L2 diff = 0.0 exactly. The two trajectories begin from the exact same canonical noise seed, which is the correct behavior. The VAE decodes noise to spatially uncorrelated color speckle.

**step_04** through **step_14** (early-to-mid denoising): By step 4, ghost structure is already visible — the rough layout of shapes and color regions has been established by the diffusion process, but everything is embedded in a heavy grain of remaining noise. By step 14, the shapes are recognizable but still quite noisy. The diff images for these early steps show moderate divergence: the two trajectories have drifted noticeably (relative L2 goes from 0% at step 0 to 2.4% by step 4, 10% by step 9, 19% by step 14), concentrated at shape edges and color boundaries. The diff is structured, not random — the disagreement has spatial coherence at the boundaries between color regions.

**step_19** through **step_29** (late denoising, refinement phase): The images become progressively sharper. Color fills solidify; the white background starts to assert itself clearly. By step 29 the composition is nearly final. The drift continues to accumulate monotonically (25% relative L2 at step 19, 29% at step 24, 31% at step 29).

**final** (fully denoised): The reference image is a clean, sharp Bauhaus composition. The reproduced image is compositionally similar but shows visible differences: shape boundaries are slightly shifted, some color fills differ, and the overall impression is of two plausible renders of the same prompt that diverged during the denoising trajectory. Relative L2 is 31.9% of the reference norm. This is not a trivial difference, but neither is it catastrophic — both images are recognizably the same scene.

### The Diff Images

The diff images (10x-amplified absolute pixel difference) are the most diagnostic artifact in this set. They are not flat noise — they have structure. At early steps, the diff is brightest at shape edges, which makes sense: small positional jitter in a boundary between two high-contrast colors produces a large per-pixel difference even if the overall composition is correct. At the final step, the diff shows the full accumulated drift: large coherent regions of neon green, cyan, and magenta outlining every shape in the composition. The diff is bright precisely where the reference image has hard edges, confirming that the divergence is edge-positional rather than a global color shift or holistic compositional disagreement.

### What the Divergence Tells Us

The divergence at step_00 is exactly zero by construction (same seed), and then error accumulates monotonically through the denoising trajectory. This growth pattern is consistent with autoregressive error amplification: each denoising step runs f(x_t, t) where the input x_t is the output of the previous step. Any epsilon of difference in the first step's output propagates and compounds.

The source of the initial divergence at step_04 is the object of study. The reference was generated by the production inference server (FP8 weights, SageAttention INT8 QK) and the reproduced trajectory was generated by a separate eager-mode rollout (src_ii/). If the reproduced run uses SDPA instead of SageAttention, or if there are any differences in the FP8 quantization path, that is where the initial epsilon originates. Even a small numerical difference at step 1 will have compounded to a 32% relative L2 error by the final step.

The fact that the final outputs are visually recognizable as the same image despite 32% relative L2 error in latent space illustrates an important property of the diffusion process: the latent-space metric is not the perceptual metric. The images share compositional identity even when their latent representations have diverged substantially. The VAE decode projects out most of the structural noise because the high-frequency latent differences cancel in the decoder's receptive fields.

### Implications for BTRM Training

This render set is the visual evidence base for the claim that the reproduced trajectory is a faithful-enough reproduction to be used in contrastive training. The final images are clearly of the same image class and would receive similar BTRM quality scores. The divergence is a measurement of numerical non-equivalence between two execution paths, not a semantic divergence. For the BTRM reward model, which discriminates on perceptual quality features (attention quantization artifacts, step count), this level of trajectory divergence is within the tolerance that makes cross-run comparisons meaningful.
